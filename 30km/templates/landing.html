When Streets
Slow Down,
Cities Come to Life

Slower traffic is more than a safety measure — it’s a tool for transforming city streets into vibrant, people-first spaces.

This platform showcases how 30km/h speed limits are transforming cities across Europe. By slowing down traffic, cities are making more room for pedestrians and cyclists, creating safer environments for everyone. Our goal is to inspire more cities to adopt these policies, improve urban life, and make streets safer for people, not just cars.

Safer Streets for All

Lower speeds significantly reduce the risk of fatal accidents, especially for pedestrians, cyclists, and children. At 30 km/h, drivers have more time to react, and crashes are far less deadly.

More Livable Cities

Slower traffic means less noise and air pollution, making neighborhoods more pleasant and encouraging walking, cycling, and outdoor activity—supporting public health and well-being.

Stronger Communities

Calmer traffic fosters better social interaction, improves local business environments, and makes streets feel more welcoming. It’s a step toward more vibrant, people-centered urban spaces.
